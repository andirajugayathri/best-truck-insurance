What is dump truck insurance

Dump truck insurance is a type of commercial vehicle insurance that protects dump truck operators from accidents, claims from other people, damage to property, and legal liability that comes from operating a dump truck. We have been in the truck insurance business for more than 25 years, and we help dump truck operators protect their assets, income, and business.

Why it's important to have dump truck insurance

  • Heavy trucks are involved in more than 8% of all fatal road crashes in Australia, even though they make up a smaller percentage of vehicles on the road.
  • Tipper and dump trucks have one of the highest rollover rates among heavy vehicles because they carry heavy loads.
  • A third-party property damage claim for a heavy vehicle can be more than AUD $120,000 on average.
  • Claims for serious injuries can go over AUD $500,000, not including legal fees.

What will happen if you work without insurance?

  • Immediate financial risk for claims and repairs
  • Losing work contracts—most sites require insured vehicles
  • The risk of going out of business because of lawsuits or unpaid claims

If you don't have dump truck insurance, one accident can cost you years of hard-earned money.

What Does Dump Truck Insurance Cover?

Vehicle Damage

  • Damage from a collision or rollover
  • Damage from fire, explosion, theft, or malicious intent
  • Costs of repairs that can be anywhere from AUD $25,000 to $150,000 or more for each incident

Third Party Property Damage

  • Damage to other vehicles, buildings, or machines
  • Claims usually range from AUD $50,000 to $300,000

Public Liability (not required but suggested)

  • Injuries to pedestrians, workers, or people on the job site
  • The usual amount of liability is between AUD $5 million and $20 million

Load and Tipping Risk (Optional)

  • Damage that happens while tipping or unloading
  • Necessary for quarry, demolition, and construction work
  • Mechanical or electrical failure that wasn't caused by an accident
  • Wear and tear (a common reason for claims to be denied)
  • Driving while drunk or without a valid license
  • Going over legal or declared limits
  • Using something without telling anyone (for example, quarry work)

More than 30% of denied truck claims are because the claimant didn't tell the truth or broke the terms of their policy. This is where expert advice comes in.
