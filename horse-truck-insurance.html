What is horse truck insurance & why is it important?

Horse truck insurance is a specialised commercial motor insurance designed for trucks and rigid vehicles used to transport horses whether for racing, breeding, agistment, events, or private transport. Designed to protect Australian owners and operators from severe financial exposure caused by accidents, injuries, or property damage.

Transporting horses is not the same as moving general freight. Horses are high value, live cargo that require specialist vehicles, experienced drivers, and the right insurance protection. With over 25 years of industry experience, we understand the real risks faced by horse truck operators across Australia.

  • The value and welfare of live animals
  • Custom-built horse floats and stalls
  • Loading, unloading, and transport risks
  • Public exposure at racetracks, events, and properties

Why horse truck insurance is important

  • A single accident involving a horse truck can result in $100,000+ in combined vehicle, liability, and animal-related claims
  • According to industry data, 1 in 5 commercial truck claims in Australia involves third-party damage or injury
  • Horse-related liability claims regularly exceed $1 million when injury or animal loss is involved

What happens if you don't have insurance?

  • Out-of-pocket vehicle repair or replacement costs
  • Legal liability for injury to third parties
  • Financial loss due to stolen or damaged trucks
  • Business shutdown due to compliance breaches
  • Personal exposure to lawsuits that can exceed seven figures

For commercial operators, driving uninsured is a business-ending risk.

What Is Covered Under Horse Truck Insurance?

Vehicle & Transport Cover

  • Accidental damage to the horse truck
  • Fire, theft, and malicious damage
  • Collision and rollover protection
  • Towing and recovery costs

Liability Protection

  • Public liability cover (commonly $10M–$20M)
  • Property damage caused during loading/unloading
  • Injury to third parties at events or properties

Business Protection

  • Hire vehicle costs
  • Downtime or loss of income
  • Custom fit-outs and horse stalls
  • Tools and equipment cover

Statistically, over 60% of horse truck claims involve loading, unloading, or reversing incidents, making liability cover essential.

  • Wear and tear or mechanical failure
  • Unroadworthy or unregistered vehicles
  • Driving under the influence of alcohol or drugs
  • Unlicensed or unauthorised drivers
  • Use outside declared business activities
  • Transporting horses not disclosed on the policy
  • Intentional acts or negligence

Understanding exclusions upfront prevents claim disputes later.
