What Is Truck Insurance?

Truck insurance is a specialised commercial motor insurance designed to protect trucks, drivers, freight, and businesses from financial loss caused by accidents, theft, damage, fires, and liability claims.

Unlike standard vehicle insurance, truck insurance is tailored for the unique demands of Australia’s transport and logistics industry. Trucks operate long hours, carry valuable cargo, travel across regional and metro routes, and face elevated risk exposure.

Significant Exclusions

Truck insurance excludes:

  • Mechanical breakdown or wear and tear
  • Unlicensed or intoxicated drivers
  • Overloading beyond legal limits
  • Incorrectly secured loads
  • Damage from illegal activities
  • Unapproved modifications
  • Pre-existing damage
  • Usage outside disclosed operations

We help you understand all exclusions clearly to avoid claim disputes.

Detailed Explanation of Each Coverage

Accidental Damage

Cost of Truck Insurance & Factors That Affect It

Premiums vary depending on your business risk profile. Key factors include:

Truck Type & Value

Heavy, specialised, or high-value vehicles cost more to insure.

Driver Age & Experience

More experienced drivers typically reduce premium costs.

Operating Radius

Interstate and long-haul operations generally carry higher risk.

Type of Cargo

Dangerous, refrigerated, or high-value goods may increase premiums.

Claims History

Fewer claims = lower premiums.

Parking & Security

Secure yards, immobilisers, cameras, and GPS tracking reduce theft risk.

Cover Level Required

Comprehensive cover offers broader protection but costs more than third-party.

Fleet Discounts

Multiple trucks may qualify for reduced rates.

We analyse all factors to deliver the most competitive and accurate premium.

Frequently Asked Questions

How much does truck insurance cost in Australia?

Truck insurance in Australia typically ranges from $3,000 to $15,000+ per year, depending on truck type, load type, driver history, routes, and business risk. Heavy haulage, tippers, and dangerous goods vehicles attract higher premiums.

Do I need comprehensive truck insurance or is third-party enough?

Third-party insurance only covers damage to someone else’s property. Most operators choose comprehensive truck insurance because it protects your truck from accidents, theft, fire, rollover, and weather-related damage.

If your truck is a major income source (owner-driver or fleet), comprehensive cover is strongly recommended.

Does truck insurance cover my trailer as well?

Only if you include Trailer in Control or Trailer Cover. Without this, your trailer may not be covered — even if the truck is.

Is cargo automatically covered under truck insurance?

No. Cargo or Goods in Transit Cover is an add-on. If you transport freight, especially high-value or specialised loads, cargo cover is essential.

Do I need public liability for truck operations?

Yes — especially if you visit customer sites, loading docks, warehouses, or construction sites. Public liability covers injury or property damage caused by your business activities outside the truck.

Can I get cover for downtime / loss of income?

Yes. Downtime Cover pays a daily benefit if your truck is off the road due to an insured event. This is one of the most common add-ons Australian operators request.

Are long-haul and interstate routes covered?

Yes, but you must disclose your operating radius. Long-haul routes (e.g., Brisbane–Melbourne, Sydney–Perth) may affect pricing and risk assessment.

Does truck insurance cover mechanical breakdown?

No. Mechanical faults, wear and tear, and engine/gearbox breakdowns are not covered. Insurance protects you from accidents and unforeseen events, not maintenance issues.

Can I insure multiple trucks under one policy?

Yes. Fleet policies are available and often offer significant premium discounts and easier management.

Do I need truck insurance if my truck stays mostly on-site?

Yes. On-site accidents, property damage, theft, and fire are still common risks. Insurance protects your asset whether it is on the road or on-site.
