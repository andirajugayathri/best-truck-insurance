What Is Truck and Trailer Insurance?

Truck and trailer insurance is designed to protect heavy vehicles, trailers, and the businesses that rely on them every day. Policies can be tailored for owner-drivers, transport companies, logistics businesses, contractors, earthmoving operators, refrigerated transport providers, and long-haul freight operators.

At the end of the day, one accident, theft, fire, storm event, or damaged load can leave your business facing downtime, repair bills, legal costs, and lost income. That is why choosing the right truck and trailer insurance policy is not simply about finding the cheapest premium. It is about finding cover that suits the way you operate.

Types of Truck and Trailer Insurance Cover

Comprehensive Cover

Comprehensive truck insurance provides the highest level of protection. It generally covers damage to your truck or trailer, third-party property damage, theft, fire, weather events, vandalism, and accidental damage.

Third Party Property Damage

Third-party property damage insurance covers damage caused to someone else’s property. This may include vehicles, buildings, fences, machinery, or infrastructure.

This type of policy does not cover damage to your own truck or trailer.

Fire and Theft Cover

Fire and theft cover protects your truck or trailer if it is stolen or damaged by fire. It can be a more affordable option for operators looking for more protection than basic third-party insurance.

Trailer in Control Cover

Trailer in control insurance can cover trailers that you do not own but are responsible for while they are attached to your vehicle.

This is particularly important for subcontractors, freight carriers, and transport businesses working with customer-owned or leased trailers.

Downtime and Loss of Income Cover

If your truck is off the road after an accident, downtime cover can help with lost income while repairs are being completed.

For many owner-drivers, this type of cover is essential because even a few days off the road can significantly impact cash flow.

Marine Transit and Goods in Transit Cover

If you transport goods for customers, goods in transit insurance can protect against loss, theft, or damage to the freight you carry.

This is especially useful for:

  • General freight operators
  • Refrigerated transport businesses
  • Furniture removals
  • Machinery transport
  • Courier companies
  • Livestock transporters
  • Construction material suppliers

Why Truck and Trailer Insurance Matters
