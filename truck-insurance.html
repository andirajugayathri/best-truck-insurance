What Is Truck Insurance?

Truck insurance is a specialised commercial motor policy designed to protect your truck, your operations, and your responsibilities on the road. Whether you run a single rigid truck or manage a fleet operating across multiple states, the right truck insurance safeguards you against accidents, theft, damage, breakdown incidents, and legal liabilities.

Australian truckies face long distances, heavy loads, unpredictable weather, and tight schedules. Having the right policy ensures one incident doesn’t halt your income or put your business at financial risk.

Did you know?

Australian truck owners lose an estimated $8,000–$25,000 every year due to uninsured downtime, roadside recovery, or towing costs after unexpected incidents.

What Does Truck Insurance Cover?

Truck insurance can be customised based on your truck type, cargo, driving radius, and business needs. Below is a detailed summary of the most common inclusions.

Comprehensive Truck Insurance

Most Popular

Covers:

  • Truck & trailer
  • Accidental damage
  • Theft or attempted theft
  • Fire, storm, hail, flood, natural disasters
  • Vandalism
  • Damage to other vehicles or property

Truck damage: up to agreed or market value ($50,000–$500,000+)

Third-party property damage: $5 million–$40 million

Personal effects: $500–$3,000

More than 40% of truck insurance claims in Australia are caused by weather events, animal strikes, and roadside incidents, not just collisions.

Liability Cover

Protects you if your truck causes damage to another vehicle, property, or causes injury.

Coverage range: $5 million–$40 million

Downtime / Loss of Income Cover

Covers your daily income while your truck is being repaired after an insured incident.

Coverage range: $300–$1,000 per day

Duration: up to 180 days

Goods in Transit / Cargo Insurance

Covers the cargo you transport from:

  • Collision damage
  • Theft in transit
  • Loading/unloading incidents

Coverage range: $10,000–$500,000+

Understanding exclusions upfront prevents claim disputes later.

  • Wear and tear, rust, or mechanical breakdown
  • Damage from overloading or unsafe loading
  • Unlicensed or unqualified drivers
  • Unroadworthy or unregistered vehicles
  • Drug or alcohol-related incidents
  • Illegal activities or carrying prohibited goods
  • Reckless or intentional acts
  • Unapproved modifications
